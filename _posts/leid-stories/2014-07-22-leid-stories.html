Global conflicts are high-priority items on the legislative agenda, and the president and his detractors in Congress are able to hammer out policy decisions and multibillion-dollar allocations to support them in a hurry.
They’re fast when they’re furious.
When it comes to domestic matters, however, it’s quite another story.
They’re deliberate in their lack of speed while social and economic indicators suggest a rapid downturn for most Americans.
Leid Stories discusses the irony.
Sen.
Elizabeth Warren (D-MA), a progressive being prodded into a 2016 presidential bid, was a keynoter at the recent Netroots Nation convention in Detroit.
Her speech to the mostly white gathering of liberal-left organizers was a clarion call for action on a number of hot-button issues.
But, says Leid Stories, Warren’s speech also pointed up a continuing problem with liberal-left politics—the refusal to acknowledge the centrality of racism to life in the United States, and the obligation of all progressive movements to condemn it and commit to fighting it.
