Leid Stories in a commentary says there’s another way to look at voting and that is, not voting.
It’s not be confused with apathy or not caring about one’s role as a good citizen.
Quite the opposite, it’s a dramatic and vigorous exercise of the checks-and-balances system in which citizens regulate the conduct of government.
Especially for groups for which voting is associated with unique histories of struggle, not voting in some circumstances can be especially strategic in compelling not only accountability in government, but in shoring up rights hard won.
