 ”Experts” wax philosophic and scientific about why he won, but won’t go so far as to unmask the real reason.
 In a no-nonsense analysis Utrice Leid deconstructs the magic political moment we’re experiencing as a nation, and precisely frames Obama’s victory within the context of America’s age-old — and all-too-familiar — construct of race.
 Obama’s first-term run and second-term victory, she contends, are “trial balloons” for beneficiaries – especially white women and white “ethnics” – who in the heat of battle steered comfortably clear of helping to build the political base he established nationwide, but, as with the civil-rights struggle and others before it, are eager to reap their unearned reward.
