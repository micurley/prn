He was a Navy Seal during the latter phase of the Vietnam War, followed by a career in the World Wrestling Federation and then a national sportscaster.
In the early 1990s, Jesse began his political career, first as mayor of Brooklyn Park in Minnesota and later being elected as the governor of the state on the independent Reform Party ticket from 1998 to 2002.
 Jesse has been an ardent supporter of Main Street Americans – when his state of Minnesota ran a budget surplus, he provided a sales tax rebate since the money belonged to the public.
He has been a proponent for gay and women’s rights, opposition to trade sanctions and a leading critic of federal policies leading America into morphing into a police surveillance state.
Jesse has written several books including  “63 Documents the Government Doesn’t Want you to Read”.
