He is one of the country’s leading environmental attorneys, and an author of several articles and books on environment, technology and society, and food issues.  Prior, Andy was senior attorney and policy director of the Greenhouse Crisis Foundation, where he organized the first Global Summit on Global Warming in 1989.
  He filed the first litigation requiring government agencies to prepare environmental impact statements on how their agencies’ actions affected ozone layer depletion and global warming.
His current work emphasizes policy and grass roots work on food issues including promoting and organic and beyond food future and opposing destructive technologies and practices such as genetic engineering, factory farming, irradiation, sewage sludge and the patenting of seeds and other life forms.
