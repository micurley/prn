Syndrome (SBS), which is sometimes known as AHT (Abusive Head Trauma).
Cases based on a ‘triad’ of bleeding in the brain, bleeding in the retina, and swelling of the brain, with no other evidence of abuse (such as bruises, grip marks, cuts, scratches or broken bones) have resulted in thousands of convictions, and there are hundreds, perhaps thousands, of people still behind bars.
  Recently there has been a shift and obtaining an SBS conviction has become more difficult.
Some of the convicted have been released from prison.
But this shameful medico-legal episode is not yet over.
    Deborah lucidly explains both the legal and medical context of SBS, and the negative interactions that occurred to produce the SBS medical dogma.
