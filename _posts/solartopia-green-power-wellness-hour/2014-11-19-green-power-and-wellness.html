We discuss Simon’s warning that without hand-counted paper ballots, we have nothing resembling a democracy in this country.

And that it will take a huge grassroots upheaval to retrieve our right to vote in any meaningful manner.
 Fitrakis and Simon point out, for example, that despite overwhelming Democratic majorities both Pennsylvania and Ohio have Congressional delegations totally dominated by Republicans.
 And that without meaningful change, it may not be worth voting at all.
Don’t miss this in-depth discussion of yet another aspect of our corrupted electoral process, and what to do about it.
